📋 Read Before Playing

Server Rules

Breaking these rules can result in a kick, ban, or character jail.

01
Random Killing (RK)
Random-killing, or RK, is the act of killing or attacking another player completely randomly, without a good reason, or for an unrealistic reason. You must have good build-up before attempting to kill someone.
02
New Life Rule (NLR)
After your in-game death, you roleplay as a new character. You cannot remember any actions leading up to your death, nor contact friends about it. You can still remember your friends, enemies, and even the person who killed you — but not the fact that they killed you. It's like it never happened. Police are immune to this rule due to cloud-backed bodycams and dashcams.
03
Gang Fear
You must have reasonable fear of gangs and large numbers. If you're outnumbered 5-to-1 on an enemy gang block, roleplay accordingly — comply with demands or attempt to flee. Police are immune to this rule as they are government-backed and can call backup, armed units, and helicopters.
04
Cop Fear
You must have reasonable fear of police and should never attempt to kill, shoot, or stab an officer. Disrespect, assault (with good reason), and evading police are permitted as they are realistic. The death rate for UK police is extremely low — attempting to murder an officer will result in character jail or a ban.
05
Exploiting
Using cheats, hacks, or any exploit is strictly forbidden. This covers every possible exploit including teleports, job hacks, kill scripts, and anything else that gives an unfair advantage.
06
Glitching
Intentionally glitching to benefit yourself is strictly forbidden. This includes glitching through walls, duping items, and any other intentional exploitation of game bugs for personal gain.
07
Metagaming
Metagaming is using out-of-character (OOC) information in-character (IC). For example: screensharing your murder to a friend so they rush to your aid. In the roleplay universe your character has no supernatural awareness of events they weren't present for. Your character is NOT aware — you are.
08
Powergaming
Powergaming is performing unrealistic actions or not giving someone a chance to react. For example: roleplaying disarming a knife off someone with no counterplay. You must allow the opposing party a chance for your action to succeed or fail — it's up to them how they react.
